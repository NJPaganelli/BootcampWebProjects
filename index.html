Design - Fashion & Related Areas
Explored a wide range of creative fields over 5 years of higher education in New York, from studio arts & jewelry making to fashion design, with additional study in textiles, millinery, handbags, corsetry, and technical/production design. Presented garments in campus wide events that were featured in FIT Museum display cases. Synthesized broader historical & contemporary themes into apparel designs, most notably in graduating student exploration of fair trade materials and their suitability for the luxury industry, which was acclaimed by the FIT Sustainability Council 2016 Conference.

Research & Design Synthesis
Researched the history of the Kashmiri shawl as a fashion and art historical garment that shows, in its migration from India to Europe, the progression of orientalist imperialism in opposite direction. Project led to further research into how contemporary design uses multicultural inspiration, and whether a form of imperialism remains in contemporary fashion industry.
